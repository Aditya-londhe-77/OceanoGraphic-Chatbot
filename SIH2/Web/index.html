    Follow a Float's Journey

    Scroll down to see how a single ARGO float travels the ocean and what it discovers.

    Float #2903829

    Indian Ocean

    Deployment

    Our journey begins in the central Indian Ocean. The float is deployed and begins its mission, descending into the depths for the first time.

    First Data Transmission

    After reaching a depth of 2,000 meters, the float surfaces and transmits its first package of data—revealing a water temperature of 4°C and a salinity of 34.7 PSU.

    Navigating the Currents

    Carried by the powerful Indian Ocean gyre, the float drifts hundreds of kilometers to the east, continuously collecting data on its silent journey.

    Surprising Discovery

    Months later, the float detects a sudden drop in salinity, indicating a large influx of freshwater, likely from a distant tropical storm. This is a key insight for climate modeling.

    From Deep Ocean to Your Screen

    Making the world's ocean data understandable for everyone.

    1. Explore the Unseen

    Thousands of robotic ARGO floats drift through the oceans, collecting a constant stream of vital data about temperature, salinity, and ocean health. This information is crucial, but it's raw, complex, and hidden in technical files.

    2. AI as Your Translator

    Our project takes this complicated data and uses advanced AI to make sense of it. FloatChat acts as your personal oceanographer, translating the numbers and code into clear, simple answers you can understand.

    3. Your Ocean, Your Questions

    Now, you can ask the questions that matter to you. Instead of complex tools, you can simply chat. Ask things like, "How warm is the Arabian Sea this month?" or "Show me the path of a float near Australia." Get instant, visual answers.
